\begin{abstract}
Non-linear structure formation provides a powerful probe of cosmological physics beyond the 
linear regime. Halo collapse, the cosmic web, and the distribution of non-linear density 
fluctuations encode information about the underlying gravitational dynamics and the microphysics 
of dark matter. In this Companion we extend the Relaxation-Driven Cyclic Cosmology (RDCC) into 
the non-linear regime and derive the consequences of relaxation-modified growth for halo 
formation, the halo mass function, concentration--mass relations, and cosmic web morphology.

Building on the scale-dependent linear growth derived in Companion~XVIII, we show that RDCC 
predicts a modified spherical and ellipsoidal collapse threshold, a relaxation-dependent 
suppression of small-scale halo abundance, and a characteristic shift in the concentration--mass 
relation. The relaxation rate $\Gamma(a)$ introduces a new physical scale that regulates the 
transition from linear to non-linear evolution, leading to a smooth modification of the halo 
mass function without invoking new particle species or exotic interactions.

We derive the RDCC-modified Press--Schechter and Sheth--Tormen formalisms, compute the 
non-linear power spectrum using a relaxation-adjusted halo model, and identify signatures in 
weak lensing, galaxy clustering, and cosmic web statistics. The predicted deviations are 
consistent with current observations and provide clear targets for future surveys such as 
Euclid, LSST, and SKA.

This Companion establishes non-linear structure formation as a key observational window into 
the relaxation dynamics of RDCC and completes the theoretical bridge from linear perturbations 
to the fully non-linear cosmic web.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation and Overview}

Non-linear structure formation is a central component of modern cosmology. While linear 
perturbation theory accurately describes the early Universe and large-scale modes, the 
formation of halos, filaments, sheets, and voids requires a fully non-linear treatment. 
Observables such as the halo mass function, the concentration--mass relation, the cosmic web 
morphology, and the non-linear matter power spectrum provide sensitive probes of the 
underlying gravitational dynamics and the microphysics of dark matter.

In the standard $\Lambda$CDM model, non-linear structure formation is governed by scale-
independent gravitational collapse. The spherical and ellipsoidal collapse thresholds are 
fixed, the halo mass function follows the Press--Schechter or Sheth--Tormen form, and the 
non-linear power spectrum can be modeled using the halo model or calibrated $N$-body 
simulations. However, several observational results suggest possible deviations from this 
simple picture:

\begin{itemize}
    \item the abundance of low-mass halos appears suppressed relative to $\Lambda$CDM 
          predictions,
    \item the concentration--mass relation shows a shallower slope than expected,
    \item cosmic web vorticity and tidal fields exhibit non-trivial scale dependence,
    \item weak lensing and galaxy clustering indicate reduced small-scale power.
\end{itemize}

These phenomena motivate the exploration of non-linear structure formation in alternative 
cosmological frameworks.

The Relaxation-Driven Cyclic Cosmology (RDCC) provides a natural mechanism for modifying

suppresses small-scale power and modifies the collapse dynamics of overdensities.

\begin{itemize}

These effects combine to produce a distinctive non-linear signature that differs from warm 
dark matter, self-interacting dark matter, and early dark energy models.

\section{RDCC-Modified Spherical and Ellipsoidal Collapse}

The transition from linear to non-linear structure formation is governed by the collapse of 
overdensities into bound halos. In the standard $\Lambda$CDM model, this process is described

\subsection*{2.1 Spherical Collapse in RDCC}

Consider a spherical overdensity with radius $R(t)$ and density contrast $\delta(t)$. The 
equation of motion is
\begin{equation}
    \ddot{R}
    = -\frac{GM(<R)}{R^2}
      + \frac{1}{3}\Lambda R
      - \Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}\, \dot{R}
      + c_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 \frac{\nabla^2 \delta}{a^2},
\end{equation}
where:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is the effective relaxation damping,
    \item $c_{\mathrm{eff}}^2$ is the effective sound speed of the combined sectors,
    \item the last term encodes the scale-dependent pressure-like contribution.
\end{itemize}

For a top-hat profile, $\nabla^2 \delta = 0$, but the effective pressure still modifies the 
background evolution of $\delta$ through the linear growth function $D(a,k)$.

The evolution of the density contrast is
\begin{equation}
    \ddot{\delta}
    + \left(2H + \Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}\right)\dot{\delta}
    - \frac{4}{3}\frac{\dot{\delta}^2}{1+\delta}
    = 4\pi G_{\mathrm{eff}}(k,a)\rho_m\, \delta(1+\delta),
\end{equation}
where $G_{\mathrm{eff}}(k,a)$ is the scale-dependent gravitational coupling derived in 
Companion~XVIII.

\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
    \sigma^2(M)

\section{RDCC Non-Linear Power Spectrum (Halo Model)}

The non-linear matter power spectrum encodes the full statistical information of the density

\section{Cosmic Web Morphology and Vorticity in RDCC}

The cosmic web is the non-linear network of filaments, sheets, voids, and nodes that emerges

% ---------------------------------------------------------
\subsection*{5.1 Tidal and Velocity-Shear Tensors in RDCC}

The tidal tensor is defined as
\begin{equation}
    T_{ij}
    = \frac{\partial^2 \Phi}{\partial x_i \partial x_j}.
\end{equation}

In RDCC, the modified Poisson equation yields
\begin{equation}
    T_{ij}^{\mathrm{RDCC}}
    = T_{ij}^{\Lambda\mathrm{CDM}}
      + \Delta T_{ij}^{\mathrm{rel}},
\end{equation}
where $\Delta T_{ij}^{\mathrm{rel}}$ arises from relaxation-induced anisotropic stress.

Similarly, the velocity-shear tensor is
\begin{equation}
    \Sigma_{ij}
    = -\frac{1}{2H}
      \left(
        \frac{\partial v_i}{\partial x_j}
        + \frac{\partial v_j}{\partial x_i}
      \right).
\end{equation}

In RDCC, the scale-dependent growth function modifies the velocity field:
\begin{equation}
    v_i(k) \propto \frac{D'(a,k)}{k^2}\, \delta(k).

Thus,
\begin{equation}
    \Sigma_{ij}^{\mathrm{RDCC}}

\end{equation}

% ---------------------------------------------------------
\subsection*{5.2 Vorticity Generation in RDCC}

The vorticity field is defined as
\begin{equation}

\end{equation}

In $\Lambda$CDM, vorticity is generated only at second order:
\begin{equation}
    \omega_{\Lambda\mathrm{CDM}} \propto \mathcal{O}(\delta^2).
\end{equation}

In RDCC, relaxation-induced anisotropic stress generates vorticity already at first order:
\begin{equation}
    \omega(k,a)
    = \frac{k}{a}\, \sigma_{\mathrm{rel}}(k,a),
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}
    \sigma_{\mathrm{rel}}
    = \frac{2}{3\rho_m}
      C_{\mathrm{rel}}\, k^2 \Delta_{\mathrm{dec}}.
\end{equation}

Thus,
\begin{equation}
    \omega_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k)
    \propto k^3 \Delta_{\mathrm{dec}}(k).
\end{equation}

This leads to enhanced vorticity on intermediate scales.

Using the RDCC expression for $\omega(k)$,
\begin{equation}
    P_{\omega}^{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k)
    = k^6 P_{\Delta_{\mathrm{dec}}}(k).
\end{equation}

Since $\Delta_{\mathrm{dec}}$ peaks near $k \sim k_{\mathrm{rel}}$, RDCC predicts:

\begin{itemize}

    \item suppressed vorticity on large scales,
    \item enhanced vorticity on intermediate scales,
    \item and a steep decline on small scales.
\end{itemize}

This is a unique RDCC signature.

the tidal or velocity-shear tensors. In RDCC, the modified tensors shift the classification 
boundaries.

Consequences:

\begin{itemize}

    \item sheets become more prominent,

\end{itemize}

This matches the reduced small-scale clustering seen in Section~4.

\section{Conclusion}

In this Companion we have extended the Relaxation-Driven Cyclic Cosmology (RDCC) into the 
fully non-linear regime of structure formation. Building on the scale-dependent linear growth 
derived in Companion~XVIII, we have shown that relaxation-induced damping, sector-dependent 
sound speeds, and anisotropic stress modify the collapse of overdensities, the abundance and 
internal structure of halos, and the morphology of the cosmic web.

The RDCC-modified spherical and ellipsoidal collapse equations lead to a scale-dependent 
collapse threshold $\delta_c(k)$, which increases on small scales and delays the formation of 
low-mass halos. This modification propagates directly into the halo mass function, producing 
a smooth suppression of low-mass halo abundance without invoking new particle species or 
exotic interactions. The concentration--mass relation is similarly affected, with RDCC 
predicting reduced concentrations for halos below the relaxation mass scale $M_{\mathrm{rel}}$.

Using a relaxation-adjusted halo model, we derived the RDCC non-linear matter power spectrum. 
The resulting suppression at $k \gtrsim k_{\mathrm{rel}}$ is smooth, physically motivated, 
and consistent with the scale-dependent linear growth. This stands in contrast to the sharp 
cutoff of warm dark matter, the halo-scale modifications of self-interacting dark matter, and 
the small-scale enhancement typical of early dark energy models.

A key prediction of RDCC is the enhancement of vorticity and the modification of tidal and 
velocity-shear fields due to relaxation-induced anisotropic stress. These effects alter the 
morphology of the cosmic web, leading to thicker filaments, larger voids, and reduced web 
connectivity. These signatures provide an independent and complementary observational window 
into the relaxation dynamics of RDCC.

Overall, the non-linear predictions developed in this Companion demonstrate that RDCC offers a 
coherent and testable framework for understanding the small-scale structure of the Universe. 
The model naturally suppresses small-scale power, modifies halo properties, and reshapes the 
cosmic web, all while remaining consistent with current observations. Future surveys such as 
Euclid, LSST, DESI, and SKA will be able to test these predictions with unprecedented 
precision.

This Companion completes the theoretical bridge from linear perturbations to the fully 
non-linear cosmic web within the relaxation-driven framework and sets the stage for future 
work on non-linear dynamics, halo assembly histories, and baryonic feedback in RDCC.
