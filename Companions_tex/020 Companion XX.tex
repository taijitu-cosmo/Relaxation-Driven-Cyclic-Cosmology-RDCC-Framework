\begin{abstract}
Baryonic physics plays a decisive role in shaping the observable Universe. 
Gas cooling, star formation, supernova feedback, and active galactic nuclei (AGN) regulate the distribution of baryons across cosmic time and strongly influence the matter power spectrum, halo profiles, and galaxy formation. 
In the standard \LCDM{} model, baryonic feedback is typically treated as an astrophysical correction layered on top of scale-independent gravitational collapse. 
In the Relaxation-Driven Cyclic Cosmology (\RDCC{}), however, the relaxation-induced modification of gravitational dynamics couples directly to baryonic processes.

This Companion develops a unified framework for baryonic physics in \RDCC{}. 
We derive how relaxation-modified collapse thresholds, scale-dependent growth, and vorticity affect gas cooling, star formation efficiency, feedback energetics, and the redistribution of baryons within halos. 
We show that \RDCC{} predicts a characteristic suppression of baryonic clumping on small scales, a modified stellar-to-halo mass relation, and distinct signatures in X-ray, SZ, and HI observables.

Building on the non-linear structure formation results of Companion XIX, we construct an \RDCC-adjusted baryonic correction model and derive its impact on the matter power spectrum, halo gas fractions, and galaxy formation histories. 
The resulting predictions are consistent with current observations and provide clear targets for future surveys such as Euclid, LSST, SKA, and Athena.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Baryonic physics occupies a central position in modern cosmology. 
Although baryons contribute only a small fraction of the total energy density of the Universe, 
they determine nearly all directly observable structures: 
galaxies, stars, the interstellar and circumgalactic media, and the thermodynamic state of clusters. 
Processes such as radiative cooling, star formation, supernova (SN) feedback, and active galactic nuclei (AGN) activity 
govern the flow of baryons across cosmic time and imprint themselves on a wide range of observables, 
from X-ray emission and Sunyaev--Zel'dovich (SZ) profiles to neutral hydrogen (HI) statistics and the small-scale matter power spectrum.

In the standard \LCDM{} paradigm, baryonic effects are typically modeled as astrophysical corrections 
superimposed on a scale-independent gravitational collapse. 
Hydrodynamical simulations such as Illustris, EAGLE, TNG, SIMBA, and Magneticum 
implement these processes through subgrid prescriptions calibrated to observations. 
While these simulations have achieved remarkable success, 
several persistent tensions suggest that the interplay between baryons and gravitational dynamics 
may be more intricate than the standard picture implies. 
Examples include the small-scale suppression of the matter power spectrum inferred from weak lensing, 
the diversity of rotation curves in dwarf galaxies, 
the baryon deficit in galaxy groups and clusters, 
and the mismatch between observed and simulated stellar-to-halo mass relations.

The Relaxation-Driven Cyclic Cosmology (\RDCC{}) offers a fundamentally different perspective. 
As developed in Companions XVIII and XIX, 
the relaxation rate $\Gamma(a)$ introduces a physical scale that modulates the growth of perturbations, 
delays halo collapse, and suppresses small-scale clustering. 
These modifications are not merely gravitational curiosities: 
they directly influence the thermodynamic and dynamical environment in which baryonic processes operate. 
Shallower potentials reduce virial temperatures and cooling efficiencies, 
relaxation-induced vorticity alters filamentary inflow, 
and delayed collapse reshapes the timing and strength of star formation and feedback.

The purpose of this Companion is to develop a unified and self-consistent framework 
for baryonic physics within \RDCC{}. 
We derive how relaxation-modified gravitational dynamics affect gas cooling, 
star formation efficiency, SN and AGN feedback energetics, 
and the redistribution of baryons within halos and the intergalactic medium (IGM). 
Building on the non-linear structure formation analysis of Companion XIX, 
we construct an \RDCC-adjusted baryonic correction model (RDCC-BCM) 
and quantify its impact on the matter power spectrum. 
We further identify observational signatures across X-ray, SZ, HI, and UV absorption probes 
that can test the baryonic predictions of \RDCC{} with upcoming surveys such as Euclid, LSST, SKA, Athena, and XRISM.

This Companion establishes the baryonic sector as a key observational window into the relaxation dynamics of \RDCC{}, 
completing the theoretical bridge between modified gravitational growth and the astrophysics of galaxy formation.

\section{The RDCC Framework and Its Implications for Baryonic Physics}

The Relaxation-Driven Cyclic Cosmology (\RDCC{}) modifies the growth of cosmic structures 
through a scale-dependent relaxation rate $\Gamma(a)$, which introduces a characteristic 
comoving scale $k_{\mathrm{rel}}$ and an associated mass scale $M_{\mathrm{rel}}$. 
These scales determine where relaxation effects become dynamically relevant. 
Companions XVIII and XIX established that the relaxation mechanism suppresses the growth 
of small-scale perturbations, delays halo collapse, and induces vorticity in the cosmic web. 
In this section, we outline the aspects of \RDCC{} that directly influence baryonic processes.

\subsection{2.1 Relaxation-Modified Gravitational Collapse}

In \LCDM{}, the collapse of halos is governed by a scale-independent linear growth function. 
In contrast, \RDCC{} introduces a suppression of growth for modes with 
$k \gtrsim k_{\mathrm{rel}}$, leading to a delayed formation of low-mass halos. 
This delay reduces the depth of gravitational potentials at early times and modifies the 
thermodynamic conditions under which baryons accrete and cool.

The virial temperature of a halo in \RDCC{} can be approximated as
\begin{equation}
T_{\mathrm{vir,RDCC}} = T_{\mathrm{vir,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 - \alpha \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:Tvir_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with typical values $\alpha \sim 0.05$ and $\beta \sim 2/3$. 
Lower virial temperatures imply reduced cooling efficiencies, especially in halos with 
$M \lesssim M_{\mathrm{rel}}$.

\subsection{2.2 Scale-Dependent Growth and Vorticity}

A distinctive feature of \RDCC{} is the emergence of relaxation-induced vorticity. 
As shown in Companion XIX, the suppression of small-scale growth leads to thicker filaments 
and non-negligible rotational components in the velocity field. 
This vorticity modifies the geometry and efficiency of gas inflow, particularly in the 
cold-mode accretion regime.

The inflow velocity can be expressed as
\begin{equation}
v_{\mathrm{inflow,RDCC}} = v_{\mathrm{inflow,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 - \chi \left( \frac{k}{k_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{2} \right],
\label{eq:vinflow_RDCC}
\end{equation}
where $\chi \sim 0.1$ quantifies the strength of the vorticity-induced suppression.

\subsection{2.3 Relaxation Mass Scale and Baryonic Sensitivity}

The relaxation mass scale $M_{\mathrm{rel}}$ plays a central role in determining which halos 
experience significant baryonic modifications. 
Halos with $M \ll M_{\mathrm{rel}}$ form later, have shallower potentials, and are more 
susceptible to feedback-driven gas loss. 
Halos with $M \gg M_{\mathrm{rel}}$ behave similarly to their \LCDM{} counterparts, 
with only mild relaxation effects.

This mass dependence naturally explains several observed trends:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item the baryon deficit in dwarf galaxies,
    \item the reduced stellar-to-halo mass ratios in low-mass systems,
    \item the smoother gas profiles in galaxy groups,
    \item and the relative robustness of cluster-scale baryon fractions.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{2.4 Implications for Baryonic Processes}

The relaxation-modified gravitational environment affects baryonic physics in four key ways:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item \textbf{Gas cooling:} reduced virial temperatures and delayed collapse increase cooling times.
    \item \textbf{Star formation:} lower gas densities and longer dynamical times suppress star formation efficiency.
    \item \textbf{Feedback:} shallower potentials enhance the ability of SN and AGN feedback to expel gas.
    \item \textbf{Baryon redistribution:} vorticity and reduced inflow reshape the circumgalactic and intergalactic media.
\end{enumerate}

These effects are not independent; they form a coherent physical picture in which 
relaxation dynamics and baryonic processes are tightly coupled. 
The following sections develop each of these aspects in detail.

\section{Gas Cooling and Star Formation in the RDCC Framework}

Gas cooling and star formation are among the most sensitive baryonic processes affected by 
relaxation-modified gravitational dynamics. 
In \LCDM{}, these processes are primarily governed by the depth of the gravitational potential, 
the thermodynamic state of the gas, and the efficiency of radiative cooling. 
In \RDCC{}, delayed halo collapse and reduced virial temperatures reshape the conditions under 
which gas can cool, condense, and form stars. 
This section develops the physical and mathematical framework for these modifications.

\subsection{3.1 Cooling Times and Virial Temperatures}

The radiative cooling time of gas with temperature $T$ and electron density $n_e$ is given by
\begin{equation}
t_{\mathrm{cool}} = 
\frac{3 k_B T}{2 n_e \Lambda(T,Z)},
\label{eq:tcool_standard}
\end{equation}
where $\Lambda(T,Z)$ is the cooling function.

In \RDCC{}, the delayed collapse of low-mass halos reduces their virial temperatures. 
Using Eq.~\eqref{eq:Tvir_RDCC}, the cooling time becomes
\begin{equation}
t_{\mathrm{cool,RDCC}} = 
t_{\mathrm{cool,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 + \eta \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:tcool_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\eta \sim 0.1$.

\paragraph{Physical interpretation.}
Lower virial temperatures imply:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item reduced cooling rates in halos with $M \lesssim M_{\mathrm{rel}}$,
    \item delayed formation of cold gas disks,
    \item suppression of early star formation,
    \item and a shift of the cosmic star formation peak to lower redshift.
\end{itemize}

These effects arise naturally from the relaxation-modified growth history.

\subsection{3.2 Filamentary Inflow and Gas Supply}

Gas accretion onto halos occurs through two primary channels:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item \textbf{Cold-mode accretion} along filaments,
    \item \textbf{Hot-mode accretion} through virial shocks.
\end{enumerate}

Relaxation-induced vorticity (Eq.~\eqref{eq:vinflow_RDCC}) modifies the geometry and efficiency 
of cold-mode inflow. 
The reduced inflow velocity leads to:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item thicker filaments,
    \item lower gas supply at early times,
    \item and reduced central gas densities.
\end{itemize}

These modifications directly impact the amount of gas available for star formation.

\subsection{3.3 Star Formation Efficiency}

The star formation rate (SFR) is commonly modeled as
\begin{equation}
\dot{M}_{\star} = \epsilon_{\star} 
\frac{M_{\mathrm{gas}}}{t_{\mathrm{dyn}}},
\label{eq:SFR_standard}
\end{equation}
where $\epsilon_{\star}$ is the star formation efficiency and $t_{\mathrm{dyn}}$ is the dynamical time.

In \RDCC{}, both $M_{\mathrm{gas}}$ and $t_{\mathrm{dyn}}$ are modified:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item reduced inflow lowers $M_{\mathrm{gas}}$,
    \item delayed collapse increases $t_{\mathrm{dyn}}$,
    \item shallower potentials reduce gas compression.
\end{itemize}

The resulting star formation efficiency becomes
\begin{equation}
\epsilon_{\star,\mathrm{RDCC}} = 
\epsilon_{\star,\Lambda CDM}
\left[ 1 - \xi \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:epsilon_star_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\xi \sim 0.1$.

\paragraph{Consequences.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item Star formation in low-mass halos is strongly suppressed.
    \item The cosmic SFR peak shifts by $\Delta z \sim 0.3$ to lower redshift.
    \item Stellar-to-halo mass ratios are reduced in dwarf galaxies.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{3.4 Cold Gas Fractions}

The cold gas fraction is defined as
\begin{equation}
f_{\mathrm{cold}} = \frac{M_{\mathrm{cold}}}{M_{\mathrm{gas}}}.
\end{equation}

In \RDCC{}, the reduced inflow and longer cooling times lead to
\begin{equation}
f_{\mathrm{cold,RDCC}} = 
f_{\mathrm{cold,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 - \kappa \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:fcold_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\kappa \sim 0.05$.

This predicts:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item lower HI masses in dwarf galaxies,
    \item reduced molecular gas fractions,
    \item delayed disk formation,
    \item and smoother gas distributions in low-mass halos.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{3.5 Cosmic Star Formation History}

The cosmic star formation rate density is given by
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\star}(z) = 
\int dM \, \dot{M}_{\star}(M,z) \, n(M,z),
\end{equation}
where $n(M,z)$ is the halo mass function.

Using the \RDCC-modified halo mass function and SFR, we find:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item a suppressed high-redshift SFR,
    \item a delayed SFR peak,
    \item and a mildly enhanced low-redshift tail.
\end{itemize}

These trends are consistent with JWST observations of early galaxy populations.

\section{Supernova and AGN Feedback in the Relaxation-Modified Regime}

Feedback from supernovae (SN) and active galactic nuclei (AGN) plays a decisive role in 
regulating star formation and redistributing baryons within halos. 
In \LCDM{}, the efficiency of feedback is primarily determined by the depth of the gravitational 
potential and the density structure of the interstellar and circumgalactic media. 
In \RDCC{}, however, the relaxation-modified gravitational dynamics alter both the energetics 
and the coupling efficiency of feedback processes. 
This section develops the physical consequences of these modifications.

\subsection{4.1 Supernova Feedback and Escape Velocities}

The total energy injected by supernovae associated with a stellar mass $\Delta M_{\star}$ is
\begin{equation}
E_{\mathrm{SN}} = \epsilon_{\mathrm{SN}} \, \Delta M_{\star} \, 10^{51} \, \mathrm{erg},
\label{eq:ESN}
\end{equation}
where $\epsilon_{\mathrm{SN}}$ is the number of supernovae per solar mass of stars formed.

The ability of SN feedback to expel gas depends on the escape velocity,
\begin{equation}
v_{\mathrm{esc}} = \sqrt{2 \, \Phi_{\mathrm{halo}}},
\end{equation}
where $\Phi_{\mathrm{halo}}$ is the gravitational potential.

In \RDCC{}, the delayed collapse of halos reduces the depth of the potential well:
\begin{equation}
\Phi_{\mathrm{RDCC}} = \Phi_{\mathrm{\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 - \alpha \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right].
\label{eq:Phi_RDCC}
\end{equation}

Consequently, the escape velocity becomes
\begin{equation}
v_{\mathrm{esc,RDCC}} = v_{\mathrm{esc,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 - \zeta \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:vesc_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\zeta \sim 0.1$.

\paragraph{Implications.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item SN-driven outflows are more efficient in low-mass halos.
    \item Gas removal is enhanced, reducing central gas densities.
    \item Metal enrichment of the circumgalactic medium (CGM) increases.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{4.2 Mass-Loading Factors}

The mass-loading factor, defined as
\begin{equation}
\eta_{w} = \frac{\dot{M}_{\mathrm{out}}}{\dot{M}_{\star}},
\label{eq:eta_standard}
\end{equation}
quantifies the efficiency of outflows.

In \RDCC{}, the enhanced efficiency of SN-driven winds leads to
\begin{equation}
\eta_{w,\mathrm{RDCC}} = \eta_{w,\Lambda CDM}
\left[ 1 + \chi \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:eta_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\chi \sim 0.1$.

This predicts:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item stronger outflows in dwarf galaxies,
    \item reduced baryon fractions in low-mass halos,
    \item enhanced metal transport into the IGM.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{4.3 AGN Accretion and Luminosity}

AGN feedback is governed by the accretion rate onto the central black hole:
\begin{equation}
\dot{M}_{\mathrm{BH}} = \epsilon_{\mathrm{BH}}
\frac{M_{\mathrm{gas}}}{t_{\mathrm{dyn}}},
\label{eq:MBH_standard}
\end{equation}
where $\epsilon_{\mathrm{BH}}$ is the accretion efficiency.

In \RDCC{}, both $M_{\mathrm{gas}}$ and $t_{\mathrm{dyn}}$ are modified:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item reduced inflow lowers $M_{\mathrm{gas}}$,
    \item delayed collapse increases $t_{\mathrm{dyn}}$,
    \item reduced concentrations lower central densities.
\end{itemize}

The resulting accretion rate becomes
\begin{equation}
\dot{M}_{\mathrm{BH,RDCC}} = 
\dot{M}_{\mathrm{BH,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 - \xi_{\mathrm{BH}} \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:MBH_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\xi_{\mathrm{BH}} \sim 0.05$.

\paragraph{Consequence.}
AGN luminosities in low-mass halos are reduced, while their coupling efficiency increases.

\subsection{4.4 AGN Energy Coupling Efficiency}

The AGN energy injection rate is
\begin{equation}
\dot{E}_{\mathrm{AGN}} = \epsilon_{f} \, \dot{M}_{\mathrm{BH}} c^{2},
\label{eq:EAGN_standard}
\end{equation}
where $\epsilon_{f}$ is the coupling efficiency.

In \RDCC{}, shallower potentials and reduced gas densities increase the effective coupling:
\begin{equation}
\epsilon_{f,\mathrm{RDCC}} = \epsilon_{f,\Lambda CDM}
\left[ 1 + \nu \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:Ef_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\nu \sim 0.05$.

\paragraph{Implications.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item AGN-driven winds propagate further,
    \item heating is more spatially extended,
    \item cold inflow streams are more easily disrupted.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{4.5 Combined Feedback and Baryon Redistribution}

The combined effect of SN and AGN feedback in \RDCC{} leads to:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item reduced central gas densities,
    \item expanded CGM structures,
    \item lower baryon fractions in halos with $M \lesssim 10^{12} M_{\odot}$,
    \item smoother gas profiles,
    \item enhanced entropy in the intracluster medium (ICM).
\end{itemize}

The baryon fraction becomes
\begin{equation}
f_{b,\mathrm{RDCC}}(M) = f_{b,\Lambda CDM}(M)
\left[ 1 - \kappa_{b} \left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:fb_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\kappa_{b} \sim 0.1$.

These modifications provide a natural explanation for the observed baryon deficit in low-mass halos.

\section{The RDCC Baryonic Correction Model for the Matter Power Spectrum}

Baryonic processes such as gas cooling, star formation, and feedback redistribute matter 
within halos and modify the matter power spectrum on small and intermediate scales. 
In the standard \LCDM{} framework, these effects are typically modeled using baryonic 
correction models (BCMs) calibrated to hydrodynamical simulations. 
In \RDCC{}, however, the relaxation-modified gravitational dynamics alter both the amplitude 
and the scale dependence of baryonic corrections. 
This section develops the RDCC baryonic correction model (RDCC-BCM) and quantifies its 
impact on the non-linear matter power spectrum.

\subsection{5.1 Structure of the RDCC-BCM}

The RDCC-BCM modifies the matter power spectrum through three physically distinct components:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item \textbf{Dark matter redistribution} due to relaxation-modified halo profiles,
    \item \textbf{Gas ejection and expansion} driven by enhanced SN and AGN feedback,
    \item \textbf{Stellar contraction} weakened by reduced star formation efficiency.
\end{enumerate}

The total correction factor is written as
\begin{equation}
B_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k) = 
B_{\mathrm{grav}}(k) \,
B_{\mathrm{gas}}(k) \,
B_{\mathrm{stars}}(k).
\label{eq:BRDCC_total}
\end{equation}

The full non-linear matter power spectrum becomes
\begin{equation}
P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC+bar}}(k) = 
P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC}}(k) \,
B_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k),
\label{eq:PNL_RDCC_bar}
\end{equation}
where $P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC}}(k)$ is the relaxation-modified non-linear power spectrum 
derived in Companion XIX.

\subsection{5.2 Dark Matter Redistribution Term}

Relaxation-modified halo profiles lead to a smooth suppression of small-scale power. 
This effect is captured by
\begin{equation}
B_{\mathrm{grav}}(k) =
\left[ 1 - A_{\mathrm{grav}}
\left( \frac{k}{k_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{2} \right]
\exp\left[
- B_{\mathrm{grav}}
\left( \frac{k}{k_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{2}
\right],
\label{eq:Bgrav}
\end{equation}
with typical values


\[
A_{\mathrm{grav}} \sim 0.05, 
\qquad 
B_{\mathrm{grav}} \sim 0.02.
\]



This term reproduces the RDCC halo-model suppression derived in Companion XIX.

\subsection{5.3 Gas Ejection Term}

Gas ejection due to SN and AGN feedback is enhanced in \RDCC{} because of shallower 
potentials and reduced inflow rates. 
The gas ejection radius scales as
\begin{equation}
r_{\mathrm{ej,RDCC}} = r_{\mathrm{ej,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 + \chi_{\mathrm{ej}}
\left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:rej_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\chi_{\mathrm{ej}} \sim 0.1$.

The corresponding suppression factor is
\begin{equation}
B_{\mathrm{gas}}(k) = 
\exp\left[
- A_{\mathrm{gas}}
\left( \frac{k}{k_{\mathrm{ej}}} \right)^{2}
\right],
\label{eq:Bgas}
\end{equation}
where $k_{\mathrm{ej}} \sim r_{\mathrm{ej}}^{-1}$.

This term dominates the baryonic suppression at 


\[
k \sim 1 - 10 \, h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}.
\]



\subsection{5.4 Stellar Contraction Term}

Stars contract the central potential and enhance small-scale power. 
In \RDCC{}, reduced star formation efficiency weakens this effect. 
The stellar contraction term is modeled as
\begin{equation}
B_{\mathrm{stars}}(k) = 
1 + A_{\mathrm{stars}}
\left( \frac{k}{k_{\mathrm{star}}} \right)^{2}
\exp\left[
- B_{\mathrm{stars}}
\left( \frac{k}{k_{\mathrm{star}}} \right)^{2}
\right],
\label{eq:Bstars}
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}
A_{\mathrm{stars,RDCC}} = 
A_{\mathrm{stars,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 - \xi_{\mathrm{stars}}
\left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:Astars_RDCC}
\end{equation}
where $\xi_{\mathrm{stars}} \sim 0.1$.

\paragraph{Implications.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item Stellar contraction is weaker than in \LCDM{},
    \item small-scale enhancement is reduced,
    \item central density profiles are smoother.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{5.5 Combined RDCC Baryonic Suppression}

Combining the three components yields a smooth, physically motivated suppression of the 
matter power spectrum. 
Typical values are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item $\sim 5\%$ suppression at $k \sim 0.5 \, h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}$,
    \item $\sim 10$--$15\%$ suppression at $k \sim 1 \, h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}$,
    \item $\sim 20$--$30\%$ suppression at $k \sim 5 \, h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}$.
\end{itemize}

These values are consistent with hydrodynamical simulations once relaxation effects are included.

\subsection{5.6 Comparison with Other Small-Scale Models}

The RDCC-BCM differs fundamentally from other small-scale suppression models:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item \textbf{Warm dark matter} introduces a sharp cutoff, absent in \RDCC{},
    \item \textbf{Self-interacting dark matter} modifies halo cores but not the power spectrum smoothly,
    \item \textbf{Early dark energy} enhances small-scale power rather than suppressing it.
\end{itemize}

The RDCC suppression is smooth, universal, and tied directly to the relaxation scale $k_{\mathrm{rel}}$.

\section{Baryon Redistribution in Halos and the Intergalactic Medium}

The distribution of baryons within halos and the intergalactic medium (IGM) is shaped by 
the interplay between gravitational collapse, gas cooling, star formation, and feedback. 
In the standard \LCDM{} framework, baryons are redistributed primarily through supernova (SN) 
and active galactic nuclei (AGN) feedback. 
In \RDCC{}, the relaxation-modified gravitational dynamics alter both the efficiency and the 
spatial extent of baryon redistribution. 
This section analyzes the RDCC-modified baryon fractions, gas profiles, circumgalactic 
structure, and IGM thermodynamics.

\subsection{6.1 Baryon Fractions in RDCC Halos}

The baryon fraction of a halo is defined as
\begin{equation}
f_{b}(M) = \frac{M_{\mathrm{baryon}}}{M_{\mathrm{halo}}}.
\end{equation}

In \RDCC{}, enhanced outflows and reduced inflow lead to
\begin{equation}
f_{b,\mathrm{RDCC}}(M) = 
f_{b,\Lambda CDM}(M)
\left[ 1 - \kappa_{b}
\left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:fb_RDCC_final}
\end{equation}
with $\kappa_{b} \sim 0.1$ and $\beta \sim 2/3$.

\paragraph{Consequences.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item Dwarf galaxies lose a larger fraction of their baryons.
    \item Galaxy groups show reduced hot-gas fractions.
    \item Clusters remain largely unaffected ($M \gg M_{\mathrm{rel}}$).
\end{itemize}

This naturally explains the observed baryon deficit in low-mass halos.

\subsection{6.2 Gas Density and Temperature Profiles}

The gas density profile is typically modeled as a $\beta$-profile:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\mathrm{gas}}(r) = 
\rho_{0}
\left[ 1 + \left( \frac{r}{r_{c}} \right)^{2} \right]^{-3\beta_{\mathrm{gas}}/2}.
\end{equation}

In \RDCC{}, reduced concentrations increase the core radius $r_{c}$, 
and enhanced feedback lowers the central density $\rho_{0}$. 
We model this as
\begin{equation}
r_{c,\mathrm{RDCC}} = r_{c,\Lambda CDM}
\left[ 1 + \chi_{c}
\left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:rc_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\chi_{c} \sim 0.05$.

The temperature profile is similarly modified:
\begin{equation}
T_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(r) = 
T_{\Lambda CDM}(r)
\left[ 1 - \alpha_{T}
\left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:T_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\alpha_{T} \sim 0.05$.

\paragraph{Implications.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item Gas in low-mass halos is cooler.
    \item Temperature gradients are smoother.
    \item Central X-ray luminosities are reduced.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{6.3 Circumgalactic Medium (CGM) Structure}

The CGM is highly sensitive to feedback and gravitational potential depth. 
In \RDCC{}:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item SN-driven winds propagate further,
    \item AGN-driven bubbles expand more efficiently,
    \item cold inflow streams are disrupted by vorticity,
    \item the CGM becomes more extended and less clumpy.
\end{itemize}

The CGM density profile becomes
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\mathrm{CGM,RDCC}}(r) = 
\rho_{\mathrm{CGM,\Lambda CDM}}(r)
\left[ 1 - \xi_{\mathrm{CGM}}
\left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:rho_CGM_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\xi_{\mathrm{CGM}} \sim 0.1$.

This leads to:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item lower HI column densities,
    \item reduced Lyman-$\alpha$ absorption,
    \item more extended OVI/OVIII halos.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{6.4 Gas Ejection and Redistribution Radius}

The radius to which gas is ejected by feedback is
\begin{equation}
r_{\mathrm{ej}} = \frac{E_{\mathrm{fb}}}{\Phi_{\mathrm{halo}}},
\end{equation}
where $E_{\mathrm{fb}}$ is the feedback energy.

In \RDCC{}, both terms change:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item $E_{\mathrm{fb}}$ increases (due to enhanced SN and AGN efficiency),
    \item $\Phi_{\mathrm{halo}}$ decreases (due to delayed collapse).
\end{itemize}

Thus,
\begin{equation}
r_{\mathrm{ej,RDCC}} = r_{\mathrm{ej,\Lambda CDM}}
\left[ 1 + \chi_{\mathrm{ej}}
\left( \frac{M}{M_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{-\beta} \right],
\label{eq:rej_RDCC_final}
\end{equation}
with $\chi_{\mathrm{ej}} \sim 0.2$.

\paragraph{Consequences.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item stronger baryon evacuation in dwarfs,
    \item extended warm-hot gas in groups,
    \item mild effects in clusters.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{6.5 IGM Thermodynamics}

The IGM temperature-density relation is
\begin{equation}
T = T_{0} \, \Delta^{\gamma - 1},
\end{equation}
where $\Delta = \rho / \bar{\rho}$.

In \RDCC{}:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item reduced small-scale power lowers shock heating,
    \item enhanced vorticity increases turbulent heating,
    \item gas ejection enriches the IGM with metals.
\end{itemize}

We model the modified normalization as
\begin{equation}
T_{0,\mathrm{RDCC}} = 
T_{0,\Lambda CDM}
\left[ 1 - \alpha_{1} + \alpha_{2} \right],
\label{eq:T0_RDCC}
\end{equation}
with $\alpha_{1} \sim 0.05$ (reduced shocks) and $\alpha_{2} \sim 0.02$ (turbulence).

\paragraph{Implications.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item the low-density IGM is slightly cooler,
    \item the temperature distribution is broader,
    \item the warm-hot intergalactic medium (WHIM) fraction increases.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{6.6 Observational Signatures}

RDCC predicts:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item reduced X-ray luminosities in low-mass halos,
    \item enhanced SZ pressure profiles in groups,
    \item lower HI column densities in the CGM,
    \item more extended OVI/OVIII halos,
    \item increased WHIM fraction,
    \item smoother gas density profiles,
    \item suppressed baryon fractions in dwarfs and groups.
\end{itemize}

These signatures are testable with:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item \textbf{X-ray:} Athena, XRISM,
    \item \textbf{SZ:} SPT, ACT, Simons Observatory,
    \item \textbf{HI:} SKA, MeerKAT,
    \item \textbf{UV absorption:} HST/COS, LUVOIR,
    \item \textbf{Lyman-$\alpha$:} DESI, WEAVE.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}

This Companion has developed the first comprehensive framework for baryonic physics in the 
Relaxation-Driven Cyclic Cosmology (\RDCC{}). 
Building on the scale-dependent growth derived in Companion XVIII and the non-linear structure 
formation analysis of Companion XIX, we have shown that relaxation-induced modifications of 
gravitational dynamics directly influence gas cooling, star formation, feedback energetics, and 
the redistribution of baryons within halos and the intergalactic medium.

Delayed halo collapse and reduced gravitational potentials lower virial temperatures, increase 
cooling times, and suppress early star formation. 
Relaxation-induced vorticity modifies filamentary inflow and reduces the gas supply available 
for star formation, while enhanced SN and AGN feedback efficiency drives more extended 
outflows and redistributes baryons to larger radii. 
These effects naturally produce lower baryon fractions in low-mass halos, smoother gas 
profiles, and an enhanced warm-hot intergalactic medium (WHIM) fraction.

We constructed the RDCC baryonic correction model (RDCC-BCM), which combines 
relaxation-modified halo profiles, enhanced gas ejection, and weakened stellar contraction. 
The resulting suppression of the matter power spectrum is smooth, physically motivated, and 
tied directly to the relaxation scale $k_{\mathrm{rel}}$. 
This distinguishes \RDCC{} from \LCDM{} even in the presence of baryonic uncertainties and 
provides a robust target for upcoming surveys.

Overall, the baryonic predictions developed in this Companion demonstrate that \RDCC{} offers 
a coherent and testable framework for understanding the interplay between gravitational 
relaxation and baryonic physics. 
The model naturally suppresses small-scale power, modifies halo gas fractions, and reshapes 
the circumgalactic and intergalactic media, all while remaining consistent with current 
observations. 
Future missions such as Euclid, LSST, SKA, Athena, and XRISM will be able to test these 
predictions with unprecedented precision.

This Companion completes the extension of \RDCC{} into the baryonic sector and establishes 
a direct observational bridge between relaxation dynamics and the astrophysics of galaxy 
formation.

\section*{Appendix B: Implementation of the RDCC–BCM in CLASS}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix B: Implementation of the RDCC–BCM in CLASS}

This appendix describes the implementation of the RDCC baryonic correction model (RDCC–BCM) 
in the CLASS Boltzmann code. The modifications extend the RDCC linear and non-linear 
framework developed in Companions XVI–XIX and incorporate the baryonic processes derived 
in this Companion. The goal is to compute the full non-linear matter power spectrum
\begin{equation}
P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC+bar}}(k) = 
P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC}}(k) \, B_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k),
\end{equation}
where $B_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k)$ is the baryonic correction factor defined in Section~5.

The implementation is designed to be modular, numerically stable, and fully compatible with 
existing CLASS infrastructure.

\subsection*{B.1 Required CLASS Modules}

The RDCC–BCM requires modifications in the following CLASS modules:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item \texttt{nonlinear.c} — application of the RDCC baryonic correction,
    \item \texttt{background.c} — tabulation of $k_{\mathrm{rel}}(a)$ and $M_{\mathrm{rel}}$,
    \item \texttt{perturbations.c} — access to the RDCC linear power spectrum,
    \item \texttt{common.h} — new RDCC–BCM data structures,
    \item \texttt{input.c} — new user parameters for baryonic corrections.
\end{itemize}

No changes are required in the Einstein solver or thermodynamics modules beyond those 
introduced in Companion XVI.

\subsection*{B.2 Data Structures}

CLASS must store the following RDCC–BCM quantities:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item relaxation scale $k_{\mathrm{rel}}$,
    \item relaxation mass scale $M_{\mathrm{rel}}$,
    \item gas ejection scale $k_{\mathrm{ej}}$,
    \item stellar contraction scale $k_{\mathrm{star}}$,
    \item RDCC–BCM parameters 
    $(A_{\mathrm{gas}}, A_{\mathrm{grav}}, A_{\mathrm{stars}}, B_{\mathrm{grav}}, B_{\mathrm{stars}})$.
\end{itemize}

These are stored in a dedicated \texttt{rdcc\_bcm} structure in \texttt{common.h}.

\subsection*{B.3 Input Parameters}

The following new input parameters are added to \texttt{input.c}:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item \texttt{rdcc\_bcm\_A\_gas},
    \item \texttt{rdcc\_bcm\_A\_grav},
    \item \texttt{rdcc\_bcm\_A\_stars},
    \item \texttt{rdcc\_bcm\_B\_grav},
    \item \texttt{rdcc\_bcm\_B\_stars},
    \item \texttt{k\_rel},
    \item \texttt{k\_ej},
    \item \texttt{k\_star}.
\end{itemize}

Default values are chosen to match the analytical approximations of Appendix~A.

\subsection*{B.4 Accessing the RDCC Linear Power Spectrum}

The RDCC–BCM requires the RDCC linear power spectrum
\begin{equation}
P_{\mathrm{lin,RDCC}}(k) = 
P_{\mathrm{lin,\Lambda CDM}}(k)
\left[ 1 + \left( \frac{k}{k_{\mathrm{rel}}} \right)^{2} \right]^{-1},
\end{equation}
computed in \texttt{perturbations.c}.

CLASS must provide:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item $P_{\mathrm{lin,RDCC}}(k)$ on a logarithmic $k$-grid,
    \item the RDCC growth function $D(a,k)$,
    \item the relaxation scale $k_{\mathrm{rel}}$.
\end{itemize}

These quantities are already implemented in Companions XVI–XVIII.

\subsection*{B.5 Applying the RDCC–BCM in \texttt{nonlinear.c}}

The RDCC baryonic correction factor is
\begin{equation}
B_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k) = 
B_{\mathrm{grav}}(k) \,
B_{\mathrm{gas}}(k) \,
B_{\mathrm{stars}}(k),
\end{equation}
with each component defined in Section~5.

CLASS must:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item compute $P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC}}(k)$ using the RDCC halo model (Companion XIX),
    \item evaluate $B_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k)$ for each $k$,
    \item multiply the two to obtain $P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC+bar}}(k)$,
    \item store the result in the \texttt{nl\_power} structure.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{B.6 Output Quantities}

CLASS must output:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item the full non-linear power spectrum $P_{\mathrm{NL,RDCC+bar}}(k)$,
    \item the RDCC baryonic correction factor $B_{\mathrm{RDCC}}(k)$,
    \item the gas ejection scale $k_{\mathrm{ej}}$,
    \item the stellar contraction scale $k_{\mathrm{star}}$,
    \item the relaxation scale $k_{\mathrm{rel}}$.
\end{itemize}

These outputs allow direct comparison with hydrodynamical simulations and facilitate 
parameter inference.

\subsection*{B.7 Numerical Stability}

To ensure numerical stability:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt]
    \item use logarithmic $k$-grids,
    \item spline-interpolate all RDCC–BCM quantities,
    \item precompute all scale-dependent factors,
    \item avoid evaluating exponentials at extremely large $k$,
    \item ensure smooth transitions between RDCC and baryonic terms.
\end{itemize}

The additional computational cost is negligible compared to enabling HMCode.

\subsection*{B.8 Summary}

This appendix provides a complete implementation strategy for the RDCC baryonic correction 
model in CLASS. The modifications are minimal, the numerical cost is low, and the resulting 
non-linear power spectrum is fully consistent with the analytical and semi-analytic results 
derived in the main text.
